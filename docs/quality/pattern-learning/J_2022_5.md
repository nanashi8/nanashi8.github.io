- Takuma is a junior high school student.
- One Saturday, / he saw a television news story / about food shortages.
- He learned / that a lot of people / around the world / can't get enough food / to eat.
- He knows / that some countries / have enough food.
- He thought, / "Why are people / in so many countries / hungry?"

- That evening / on the Internet, / Takuma studied / about food shortages.
- He learned / that one of the reasons / for food shortages / is bad weather.
- In some countries, / it doesn't rain much / or it rains too much.
- The people / who live in such countries / can't get enough food.
- When it doesn't rain, / farm products don't grow well.
- When it rains too much, / the products are damaged.
- It isn't easy / for the people / to get food.

- The next day, / Takuma went to the library / near his house / to learn more / about food shortages.
- He read some books / about the food problem.
- From one book, / he learned / that there is another reason.
- People / in some countries / are so poor / that they can't buy enough food / to eat.
- He was sad / to learn that.

- That evening, / Takuma told his parents / and his grandmother / about that.
- His grandmother said, / "Takuma, / you know / there is a lot of food / to eat / in Japan.
- We can eat enough food / every day.
- When we eat dinner / at home, / your mother often tells you / to eat all the food / on your plate."
- He thought / his grandmother was right.
- He said to his mother, / "OK, Mom, / I will try to eat all the food / on my plate / because I don't want to waste food."

- On Monday, / at school / he made a speech / in class / about food shortages.
- He said, / "What can we do / to help hungry people / around the world?
- There are ways / to help them.
- For example, / we can send them food / because we have a lot of food / in Japan.
- You think / sending food isn't easy, / right?
- Then we can do more.
- It's sending money / to them.
- Sending money / is easier than / sending food.
- We can look for other ways / to help hungry people / around the world / on the Internet / or in books.
- We should also try / to learn the causes / of the food problem."

- Now, / Takuma is more interested / in the food problem / of the world.
- He wants the students / of his school / to think about the problem / with him.
- He decided / to make a school newspaper / and write about it.
- He thinks, / "More students / of my school / will start to become interested / in the problem / if they read about it / in the school newspaper.
- And I hope / we can help hungry people / in poor countries.
- We can do many things / to help them."

## 4. 一文訳

- Takuma is a junior high school student.
  タクマは中学生です。
- One Saturday, he saw a television news story about food shortages.
  ある土曜日、彼は食料不足についてのテレビニュースを見ました。
- He learned that a lot of people around the world can't get enough food to eat.
  彼は世界中の多くの人々が十分な食べ物を得られないことを知りました。
- He knows that some countries have enough food.
  彼はいくつかの国には十分な食べ物があることを知っています。
- He thought, "Why are people in so many countries hungry?"
  彼は考えました、「なぜこんなに多くの国で人々は飢えているのだろう？」

- That evening on the Internet, Takuma studied about food shortages.
  その日の夕方、インターネットで、タクマは食料不足について調べました。
- He learned that one of the reasons for food shortages is bad weather.
  彼は食料不足の理由の一つが悪天候であることを学びました。
- In some countries, it doesn't rain much or it rains too much.
  いくつかの国では、雨があまり降らないか、または雨が降りすぎます。
- The people who live in such countries can't get enough food.
  そのような国に住む人々は十分な食べ物を得られません。
- When it doesn't rain, farm products don't grow well.
  雨が降らないとき、農作物はよく育ちません。
- When it rains too much, the products are damaged.
  雨が降りすぎると、作物は傷んでしまいます。
- It isn't easy for the people to get food.
  人々が食べ物を得るのは簡単ではありません。

- The next day, Takuma went to the library near his house to learn more about food shortages.
  次の日、タクマは食料不足についてもっと学ぶために家の近くの図書館に行きました。
- He read some books about the food problem.
  彼は食料問題についての本を何冊か読みました。
- From one book, he learned that there is another reason.
  一冊の本から、彼は別の理由があることを学びました。
- People in some countries are so poor that they can't buy enough food to eat.
  いくつかの国の人々はとても貧しいので、十分な食べ物を買うことができません。
- He was sad to learn that.
  彼はそれを知って悲しくなりました。

- That evening, Takuma told his parents and his grandmother about that.
  その日の夕方、タクマは両親と祖母にそのことを話しました。
- His grandmother said, "Takuma, you know there is a lot of food to eat in Japan.
  祖母は言いました「タクマ、日本には食べるものがたくさんあることを知っているわね。
- We can eat enough food every day.
  私たちは毎日十分な食べ物を食べることができます。
- When we eat dinner at home, your mother often tells you to eat all the food on your plate."
  家で夕食を食べるとき、お母さんはよくあなたに皿の上の食べ物を全部食べるように言っているでしょう。」
- He thought his grandmother was right.
  彼は祖母が正しいと思いました。
- He said to his mother, "OK, Mom, I will try to eat all the food on my plate because I don't want to waste food."
  彼は母に言いました、「わかったよ、お母さん。食べ物を無駄にしたくないから、お皿の上の食べ物を全部食べるようにするよ。」

- On Monday, at school he made a speech in class about food shortages.
  月曜日、学校で彼はクラスで食料不足についてスピーチをしました。
- He said, "What can we do to help hungry people around the world?
  彼は言いました、「世界中の飢えている人々を助けるために私たちに何ができるでしょうか？
- There are ways to help them.
  彼らを助ける方法があります。
- For example, we can send them food because we have a lot of food in Japan.
  例えば、日本には食べ物がたくさんあるので、彼らに食べ物を送ることができます。
- You think sending food isn't easy, right?
  食べ物を送るのは簡単ではないと思いますよね？
- Then we can do more.
  それならもっとできることがあります。
- It's sending money to them.
  それは彼らにお金を送ることです。
- Sending money is easier than sending food.
  お金を送るのは食べ物を送るより簡単です。
- We can look for other ways to help hungry people around the world on the Internet or in books.
  インターネットや本で世界中の飢えている人々を助ける他の方法を探すことができます。
- We should also try to learn the causes of the food problem."
  私たちは食料問題の原因についても学ぶべきです。」

- Now, Takuma is more interested in the food problem of the world.
  今、タクマは世界の食料問題にもっと興味を持っています。
- He wants the students of his school to think about the problem with him.
  彼は学校の生徒たちに彼と一緒にこの問題について考えてほしいと思っています。
- He decided to make a school newspaper and write about it.
  彼は学校新聞を作ってそれについて書くことに決めました。
- He thinks, "More students of my school will start to become interested in the problem if they read about it in the school newspaper.
  彼は考えています、「もし学校新聞でそれについて読めば、学校のもっと多くの生徒がこの問題に興味を持ち始めるでしょう。
- And I hope we can help hungry people in poor countries.
  そして私は貧しい国の飢えている人々を助けられることを願っています。
- We can do many things to help them."
  私たちは彼らを助けるために多くのことができます。」
