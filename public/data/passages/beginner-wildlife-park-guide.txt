Wildlife Park Guide - Visitor Information

Featured Animals

Farm Animals Section

Meet traditional farm animals including cows, pigs, and chickens. This interactive area allows visitors to observe daily farming activities. Our farmer staff demonstrates milking cows, feeding pigs, and collecting eggs.

"Many children have never seen real farm animals," explains Head Farmer Johnson. "This hands-on experience helps them understand where food comes from and appreciate agricultural work."

Kids especially love the baby animals. Currently, we have piglets, calves, and chicks that visitors can gently touch under staff supervision. Please follow all instructions to ensure both visitor and animal safety.

Rainforest Habitat

Our tropical section features gorillas, frogs, and various bird species. The climate-controlled environment replicates their natural habitat with authentic vegetation and water features.

The gorilla family is particularly popular. Visitors watch them play, eat, and interact through large viewing windows. Educational signs explain their behaviors, diet, and conservation status.

"Gorillas are incredibly intelligent and emotional," notes Primate Specialist Dr. Martinez. "Observing them helps people understand how closely related we are. Protecting their wild habitat is crucial for survival."

The frog exhibit showcases over thirty species, from tiny tree frogs to large bullfrogs. Some display brilliant colors as warning signals. Interactive displays explain their role in ecosystems and environmental threats they face.

Australian Animals

The koala habitat is always crowded with admiring visitors! These adorable marsupials spend most of their time sleeping in eucalyptus trees. Feeding time at 2 p.m. daily is the best opportunity to see them active.

"Koalas are surprisingly picky eaters," explains Australian Section Manager Sarah. "They only consume certain eucalyptus species. We import fresh leaves weekly to ensure proper nutrition."

Our kangaroo area allows visitors to walk through their enclosure safely. These gentle creatures often approach people curiously. Staff provides special food so visitors can feed them under supervision.

Wildlife Conservation Center

Behind the scenes, our dedicated researchers and veterinarians work daily to protect endangered species. Current projects include breeding programs for threatened animals and habitat restoration initiatives.

Dr. Chen, our lead vet, treats injured wild animals brought to the facility. "We've successfully rehabilitated and released over fifty animals this year," she reports proudly. "Each animal saved makes a difference for species survival."

Visitors can observe some conservation work through viewing windows at the Wildlife Center. Educational presentations explain research methods and conservation challenges. Programs run at 11 a.m. and 3 p.m. daily.

Special Programs & Events

Keeper Talks (Free with admission)
Professional animal keepers share fascinating facts about their animals. Learn feeding habits, behaviors, and conservation status. Schedule posted at park entrance.

Professional photographer Mr. Thompson teaches techniques for capturing amazing animal photos. Monthly workshops ($25 plus admission). Participants learn composition, lighting, and equipment usage. Registration required.

Junior Zookeeper Program
Children aged 8-14 can participate in this popular summer program. Activities include feeding animals, cleaning habitats, and learning veterinary basics. Applications open in March. Space is extremely limited.

Birthday Parties
Celebrate at the park! Party packages include admission, private room use, and special animal encounter. The birthday child meets a small animal like a rabbit or guinea pig up close. Prices start at $200 for ten guests.

Safety Guidelines

For everyone's safety and animal welfare, please follow these rules:

• Stay on designated paths
• Don't climb fences or barriers
• No feeding animals except in supervised areas
• Keep children supervised at all times
• No running, loud noises, or disturbing animals
• Don't tap on glass or throw objects
• Proper shoes required (no sandals in some areas)

Violation of rules may result in removal from the park without refund. We appreciate your cooperation in maintaining a safe, enjoyable environment for everyone.

Plan Your Visit

Most visitors spend 3-4 hours exploring. Wear comfortable shoes—there's lots of walking! Bring sun protection during summer months. Weather can change quickly, so layers are recommended.

Maps available at entrance showing animal locations, restrooms, dining areas, and show times. Mobile app available for download with interactive map and real-time updates.

Volunteer and Support Programs

Volunteer Opportunities

Greenwood Wildlife Park relies heavily on dedicated volunteers who donate their time and energy. Volunteers assist with various duties including:

**Guest Services**: Greeting visitors at entrances, distributing maps and brochures, answering frequently asked questions about exhibits and facilities. Volunteers help create welcoming first impressions and ensure guests navigate the park efficiently.

**Educational Programs**: Assisting during school field trips, helping with hands-on activities, supervising children during interactive demonstrations. Educational volunteers need patience and enthusiasm for working with young learners.

**Habitat Maintenance**: Helping groundskeepers maintain landscaping, cleaning pathways, planting gardens that attract beneficial insects and birds. This physical work keeps the park beautiful and ecologically healthy.

**Special Events**: Supporting fundraising galas, community festivals, holiday celebrations, and promotional activities. Event volunteers handle tasks from setting up decorations to managing parking and registration.

**Animal Care Support**: Under strict supervision by licensed professionals, advanced volunteers may assist with preparing animal meals, cleaning habitats, and observing animal behavior for research purposes. This position requires extensive training and demonstrated commitment.

Volunteers receive benefits including free park admission, exclusive behind-the-scenes tours, invitations to special volunteer appreciation events, and the satisfaction of supporting vital conservation work.

"Our volunteers are invaluable," emphasizes Volunteer Coordinator Maria Gonzalez. "We simply couldn't operate without their generous assistance. Many volunteers continue for years, becoming beloved members of our community."

To apply for volunteer positions, prospective candidates complete an application form available on our website. We conduct interviews and background checks to ensure safety. Training sessions occur monthly for new volunteers.

Conservation Education Initiatives

School Partnership Programs

We've developed comprehensive educational curricula aligned with state science standards. Schools can schedule guided tours where trained educators lead students through exhibits while explaining biology, ecology, and environmental science concepts.

Interactive workshops allow students to examine animal artifacts like feathers, bones, and shells. They learn scientific observation and classification methods. Older students conduct actual research projects, collecting behavioral data on specific species.

"These programs inspire the next generation of conservationists," observes Education Director Dr. Patricia Lee. "Many students visit Greenwood during elementary school and later pursue careers in biology, veterinary medicine, or environmental science. We're planting seeds for future environmental stewardship."

Teachers receive free preparatory materials including lesson plans, vocabulary lists, and activity suggestions. Post-visit resources help educators reinforce concepts back in classrooms. We've served over 15,000 students annually through these initiatives.

Community Outreach

Greenwood staff regularly present programs at libraries, community centers, and senior facilities. We bring educational animals—typically gentle species like rabbits, turtles, or birds—and discuss wildlife topics. These outreach efforts extend our mission beyond park boundaries.

We collaborate with local environmental organizations on conservation campaigns. Recent partnerships focused on reducing plastic pollution, protecting wetland habitats, and creating wildlife corridors through urban areas. Community engagement amplifies our impact significantly.

Our annual "Wildlife Festival" celebrates biodiversity through activities, performances, and educational booths. Local artists display nature-inspired artwork. Environmental groups promote conservation initiatives. The festival attracts over 10,000 attendees, raising awareness while generating funds for our programs.

Research and Conservation Projects

Breeding Programs

Several species in our collection participate in cooperative breeding programs coordinated among zoos worldwide. These programs maintain genetically diverse populations of endangered species as insurance against extinction.

Our successful gorilla breeding program has produced six offspring over fifteen years. These births represent significant achievements given gorillas' slow reproduction rates. Some offspring have transferred to other facilities, contributing to genetic diversity across the population.

"Coordinating these programs requires international cooperation," explains Conservation Biologist Dr. James Wright. "We analyze genetic data to determine optimal breeding pairs. We share our successes and learn from challenges. Collectively, we're ensuring species survival."

Habitat Restoration

Beyond our facility, Greenwood supports field conservation projects protecting wild habitats. Recent initiatives include:

**Wetland Protection**: Funding acquisition of endangered wetlands critical for migrating waterfowl. These areas provide nesting sites and food resources for numerous species.

**Reforestation Projects**: Planting native trees in deforested regions. Trees provide habitat while combating climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide.

**Anti-Poaching Efforts**: Supporting ranger patrols in African reserves protecting elephants and rhinos from illegal hunting. We've donated vehicles, communication equipment, and training resources.

**Marine Conservation**: Contributing to coral reef restoration and sea turtle protection programs. Ocean ecosystems face severe threats from pollution, overfishing, and climate change.

Portion of admission fees and all profit from merchandise sales fund these external projects. Visitors contribute to global conservation simply by enjoying their park experience.

Looking Forward

Greenwood Wildlife Park continuously evolves to better serve animals, visitors, and conservation goals. Upcoming developments include:

**Arctic Habitat Expansion**: Construction begins next spring on a new polar bear and penguin exhibit featuring innovative climate control technology and underwater viewing areas.

**Nocturnal Animals Building**: Specialized facility with reversed lighting enabling visitors to observe normally elusive nighttime animals during daytime visits.

**Interactive Technology**: Augmented reality apps that overlay educational information when visitors point smartphones at exhibits. Digital components complement traditional learning methods without replacing authentic animal encounters.

**Sustainability Improvements**: Installing additional solar panels, upgrading to energy-efficient systems, implementing comprehensive recycling and composting programs. We're committed to minimizing our environmental footprint.

Your continued support makes these ambitious projects possible. Together, we're creating a world where humans and wildlife thrive together, where children develop deep appreciation for nature, and where endangered species receive protection they desperately need.
